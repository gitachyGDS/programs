1. Information Technology
Information Technology
IT course typically spans over three years and covers a range of subjects including programming languages, database management, web development, networking, hardware maintenance, and software engineering. The curriculum is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of information technology and its applications in various fields. Additionally, practical training and internships are often included to ensure students gain hands-on experience in the IT industry.
2. Geomatics Engineering
Geomatics Engineering
A three-year Geomatics Engineering course offered by CTEVT (Council for Technical Education and Vocational Training) provides students with a comprehensive understanding of geospatial technology and its applications in various fields. In the initial year, students delve into fundamental subjects such as Engineering Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Computer Applications. These courses establish a strong foundation by imparting essential mathematical concepts, principles of physics and chemistry, and introductory computer skills necessary for geomatics engineering.
3. Civil Engineering
Civil Engineering
A three-year Civil Engineering course offered by CTEVT (Council for Technical Education and Vocational Training) equips students with a comprehensive understanding of the principles, theories, and practical applications of civil engineering. In the first year, students typically delve into foundational subjects such as Engineering Mathematics, Engineering Physics and Chemistry, Engineering Drawing, Engineering Mechanics, and Computer Applications. These subjects lay the groundwork for subsequent learning by providing a solid understanding of mathematical concepts, physical principles, drafting techniques, and introductory computer software relevant to civil engineering.
